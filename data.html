The raw dataset used is sourced from the anonymous survey answers provided by MPF. This dataset includes a variety of question responses, and while some are hardly related to the topic of our analysis, many are quite useful (to be discussed later) in particular with regards to targeting alumni involvement opportunities. The most noteworthy quality of this dataset is the fact that none of it is quantitative; all columns contain data that is either ordinal, categorical, or what we will refer to as “Text Box Responses.” In other words, quantitative analysis and visualizations depicted below are made possible by using the counts of aforementioned data types.
As briefly mentioned above, we found that certain columns contained data that was either unrelated to the subject of analysis, or made impossible to use due to formatting issues. In particular, the columns containing Text Box Responses were far too varied and uncategorized to be of much use. Thus, for all intensive purposes we were able to cut out a significant portion of the original dataset. This decision was made to preserve only the data that we hypothesized would show statistical correlation with respect to our analytical goal, thereby allowing us to clean and store relevant data more easily and efficiently. The key remaining data consists of Likert scale responses (Strongly Disagree - Strongly Agree), binary responses (Yes/no or Yes/N/A), and categorical responses (such as field of work).
