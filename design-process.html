We chose to prioritize the above tasks for our final visualization. Every aspect of our design is specifically chosen to effectively execute each of the prioritized tasks. For example, tasks 2, 3, 4, and 5 can all be addressed by considering the bar chart visualization, in either its past or future form. Task 1, on the other hand, can be consistently completed regardless of the state of the bar chart simply by viewing the current state of the pie chart.
Our visualization uses only area as a mark, and uses color and size as channels. These are both quintessential to our design, since based on the tasks we chose and the visualizations we finalized from the initial sketches, each of these is necessary. Another key part of the design is the switch, or button, which alternates the bar chart from depicting data from past alumni events and data regarding possible future events. This interactive element is crucial because without it, we wouldn’t be able to effectively address all tasks regarding participation (or desire to participate) in alumni events.
